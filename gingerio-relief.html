Relief
one step at a time
Time: Aug. 2015 - Nov. 2015
I designed a series of 2-to-5-minute mental health activities that people can practice anytime, anywhere on their phones to learn and build depression coping skills.
Some background
Ginger.io collects behavioral data from user’s phone and employ a number of algorithms to detect changes that may indicate mental health symptoms so we can provide care to users exactly when needed.
The problem
Users come to Ginger.io seeking help for depression, but our current app does not provide them sufficient self-management tools to cope with and reduce depression symptoms. We started from listening to our users.
We wanted to know more about how the health tips helped people. So we asked:

We realized the problem was not insufficient health tips, but insufficient support in learning new skills and forming new habits.





Step 1
Digging deep into research
User research key findings:

1. Ease of use and low cost are the most important factors
2. Depression makes behavior change even harder
3. Treatment needs to fit into busy schedules
4. Anxiety and negative thoughts are the two main probems people want help with

Therapist interview key findings:

1. 1. No single technique works for everyone
2. Frustration usually comes before confidence
3. Regular daily schedules keep depression in check

We also tried lots of therapies ourselves, including Cognitive Behavioral Therapy and Acceptance and Commitment Therapy.
